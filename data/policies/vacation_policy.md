# Vacation Policy
**Company:** Northstar Analytics, Inc.  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2026

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to provide employees with planned time away from work to rest, manage personal commitments, and maintain work-life balance.

## 2. Eligibility
This policy applies to all full-time employees. Part-time employees may receive prorated vacation benefits if approved by Human Resources and permitted by local law.

## 3. Annual Vacation Allowance
Full-time employees receive paid vacation based on length of service:
- **0 to 2 years of service:** 15 vacation days per year
- **3 to 5 years of service:** 20 vacation days per year
- **6 or more years of service:** 25 vacation days per year

Vacation is accrued monthly and becomes available as it is earned.

## 4. Request Process
Employees should submit vacation requests through the company HR or scheduling system at least:
- **2 weeks in advance** for absences of 1 to 3 days
- **4 weeks in advance** for absences longer than 3 days

Managers should review requests based on staffing needs, project deadlines, and fairness across the team.

## 5. Approval Rules
Vacation is not considered approved until the employee receives confirmation from their direct manager or the HR system. Employees should not make non-refundable travel arrangements until leave has been approved.

## 6. Carryover
Employees may carry over up to **5 unused vacation days** into the next calendar year. Any unused days beyond that limit are forfeited unless local law requires a different approach.

## 7. Blackout Periods
Certain business-critical periods may be designated as vacation blackout periods for specific teams. Managers must communicate such periods in advance whenever possible.

## 8. Sick Leave vs. Vacation
Vacation time is intended for planned personal time off. Employees who are ill should use the appropriate sick leave policy or notify management according to absence procedures.

## 9. Separation from Employment
Unused accrued vacation may be paid out at separation if required by law or company policy. Any advanced but unearned vacation may be deducted from final pay where legally allowed.

## 10. Policy Questions
Questions about accrual balances, approvals, or carryover should be directed to Human Resources.

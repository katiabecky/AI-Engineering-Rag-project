# Code of Conduct
**Company:** Northstar Analytics, Inc.  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2026

## 1. Purpose
Northstar Analytics expects all employees to act ethically, respectfully, and professionally. This Code of Conduct defines the standards that guide our workplace behavior and business decisions.

## 2. Professional Respect
Employees must treat coworkers, clients, partners, and vendors with courtesy and respect. Harassment, intimidation, discrimination, retaliation, and abusive behavior are not acceptable.

## 3. Integrity and Honesty
Employees must provide accurate information, act honestly in business matters, and avoid misleading statements, falsified records, or deceptive conduct.

## 4. Conflicts of Interest
Employees must avoid situations in which personal interests interfere, or appear to interfere, with the interests of the company. Any actual or potential conflict of interest must be disclosed to management or Human Resources.

## 5. Confidential Information
Employees must protect confidential company, employee, and customer information. Confidential material may not be disclosed, copied, or used for personal gain.

## 6. Anti-Harassment Commitment
Northstar Analytics prohibits harassment of any kind, including verbal, written, visual, physical, or online misconduct. Employees who witness or experience harassment should report it immediately.

## 7. Use of Company Resources
Employees must use company time, systems, funds, and materials responsibly and only for legitimate business purposes, except where limited personal use is explicitly permitted.

## 8. Reporting Concerns
Employees are encouraged to report concerns about unethical conduct, policy violations, fraud, harassment, or safety issues. Reports may be made to a manager, Human Resources, or any approved reporting channel.

## 9. Non-Retaliation
No employee will be penalized for reporting a concern in good faith or participating in an investigation.

## 10. Disciplinary Consequences
Violations of this Code of Conduct may lead to coaching, written warnings, suspension, or termination depending on the seriousness of the issue.

# Remote Work Policy
**Company:** Northstar Analytics, Inc.  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2026

## 1. Purpose
Northstar Analytics supports flexible work arrangements where business needs allow. This policy explains eligibility, availability, and expectations for remote work.

## 2. Eligibility
Remote work is available only for roles that can be effectively performed off-site without reducing service quality, security, or team coordination. Eligibility is determined by management based on job function, performance history, and operational requirements.

## 3. Standard Arrangement
The standard remote work arrangement is **up to three remote days per week** for eligible employees. Team leaders may require specific in-office collaboration days.

## 4. Approval Process
Employees must submit a remote work request to their manager. The request should describe:
- the proposed work schedule,
- the primary remote work location,
- internet reliability,
- availability during business hours.

Final approval requires manager confirmation and may be reviewed by Human Resources or IT when needed.

## 5. Work Hours and Availability
Remote employees are expected to remain available during standard business hours, attend required meetings, respond to messages in a timely manner, and maintain the same productivity expectations as on-site employees.

## 6. Workspace Requirements
Employees working remotely must have:
- a quiet and professional workspace,
- reliable high-speed internet,
- access to approved company hardware,
- the ability to protect confidential information.

## 7. Security Requirements
All remote work must comply with the IT Security Policy. Employees must use company-approved devices, strong passwords, multi-factor authentication, and secure connections. Public Wi-Fi may not be used without company-approved VPN protection.

## 8. Working Outside the Approved Location
Employees may not regularly work from a different city or country without written approval. Cross-border remote work may create tax, legal, data privacy, and security concerns.

## 9. Equipment and Support
The company may provide a laptop, charger, and approved accessories for business use. Employees are responsible for taking reasonable care of all equipment and reporting damage, loss, or theft immediately.

## 10. Revocation of Remote Work Privileges
Remote work is a business privilege, not a guaranteed right. The company may modify or revoke remote work arrangements at any time based on performance, role changes, security issues, or operational needs.

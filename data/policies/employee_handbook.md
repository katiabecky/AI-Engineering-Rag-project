# Employee Handbook
**Company:** Northstar Analytics, Inc.  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2026

## 1. Welcome
Welcome to Northstar Analytics, Inc. This handbook provides a general overview of our workplace expectations, employee benefits, communication norms, and day-to-day policies. It is intended to help employees understand how we work together as a professional, respectful, and accountable organization.

## 2. Employment Status
Northstar Analytics classifies employees as full-time, part-time, temporary, or contract workers.
- **Full-time employees** are scheduled to work 40 hours per week and are generally eligible for the full benefits package.
- **Part-time employees** work fewer than 30 hours per week and may receive limited benefits depending on local law and company policy.
- **Temporary employees** are hired for a limited project or timeframe.
- **Contract workers** are not company employees and are managed according to vendor or contract terms.

## 3. Standard Work Hours
Standard business hours are **9:00 a.m. to 5:30 p.m., Monday through Friday**, with a 30-minute unpaid lunch break. Employees are expected to be available and responsive during their assigned working hours unless approved otherwise.

## 4. Attendance and Punctuality
Employees are expected to report to work on time and maintain reliable attendance. If an employee will be late or absent, they must notify their direct manager as early as possible and no later than **30 minutes before the start of the workday**.

Unplanned absences of more than **three consecutive workdays** may require supporting documentation.

## 5. Equal Opportunity and Respectful Workplace
Northstar Analytics is committed to equal opportunity and does not tolerate discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, or any other protected status. We are committed to a workplace where employees are treated with dignity and respect.

## 6. Communication Expectations
Employees should use company communication tools professionally and respectfully. Important work decisions should be documented in writing through approved channels such as email, internal chat, project management systems, or meeting notes.

## 7. Performance and Feedback
Employees participate in a formal performance review process **twice per year**, typically in June and December. Managers are expected to provide regular feedback, discuss goals, and identify professional development opportunities.

## 8. Workplace Conduct
Employees must act professionally, honestly, and respectfully in all business interactions. Bullying, harassment, threats, dishonesty, misuse of company property, or violation of company policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

## 9. Use of Company Property
Company laptops, systems, email accounts, and collaboration platforms are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use may be permitted if it does not interfere with work, create security risks, or violate company policy.

## 10. Confidentiality
Employees may have access to confidential business information, customer records, financial information, product strategies, and internal operational data. Confidential information must not be disclosed to unauthorized persons during or after employment.

## 11. Health and Safety
Employees must follow all workplace safety instructions and report unsafe conditions immediately. Any work-related injury, safety concern, or security incident must be reported to a manager or Human Resources as soon as possible.

## 12. Questions About Policies
This handbook summarizes major employment expectations, but more detailed rules may appear in separate policy documents such as the Vacation Policy, Remote Work Policy, IT Security Policy, Code of Conduct, and Expense Reimbursement Policy.

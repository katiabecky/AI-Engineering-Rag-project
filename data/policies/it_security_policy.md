# IT Security Policy
**Company:** Northstar Analytics, Inc.  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2026

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes the minimum information security requirements for protecting company systems, customer data, confidential information, and digital assets.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, interns, and temporary staff who access company systems, devices, or data.

## 3. Password Requirements
All employees must use strong passwords that:
- contain at least **12 characters**,
- include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols,
- are not reused across personal and business accounts.

Passwords must be changed immediately if compromise is suspected.

## 4. Multi-Factor Authentication
Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all company email, cloud platforms, and business-critical applications where supported.

## 5. Device Use
Employees must use company-approved devices for business work unless written authorization is granted. Devices must be protected with screen locks, antivirus tools, current security updates, and full-disk encryption where configured by IT.

## 6. Email and Phishing
Employees must remain alert to suspicious emails, links, attachments, and social engineering attempts. Suspected phishing messages should be reported to IT immediately and should not be opened, forwarded, or replied to.

## 7. Data Handling
Confidential or sensitive information may only be stored, transmitted, or shared using approved company systems. Employees must not upload confidential files to personal storage accounts or unapproved third-party tools.

## 8. Public Wi-Fi and Remote Access
When working remotely, employees must avoid unsecured public Wi-Fi unless using a company-approved VPN. Sensitive work should not be conducted in a way that exposes company information to unauthorized viewing.

## 9. Access Control
Employees may access only the systems and information necessary for their job responsibilities. Sharing passwords or allowing another person to use one's account is strictly prohibited.

## 10. Incident Reporting
Any suspected security incident, including lost devices, phishing attempts, malware, unauthorized access, or accidental data exposure, must be reported to IT within **one hour of discovery**.

## 11. Violations
Violations of this policy may result in loss of system access, disciplinary action, or termination, depending on severity.

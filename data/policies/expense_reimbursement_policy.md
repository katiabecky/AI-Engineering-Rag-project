# Expense Reimbursement Policy
**Company:** Northstar Analytics, Inc.  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2026

## 1. Purpose
This policy explains which business expenses may be reimbursed and how employees should submit reimbursement requests.

## 2. Eligible Expenses
Reasonable, necessary, and business-related expenses may be reimbursed when properly documented. Eligible expenses may include:
- approved business travel,
- lodging,
- meals during business travel,
- local transportation,
- client meeting expenses,
- approved office supplies purchased for business use.

## 3. Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The following are generally not reimbursable unless expressly approved in advance:
- personal entertainment,
- traffic fines or parking penalties,
- alcohol not connected to an approved client event,
- expenses for spouses, family members, or guests,
- upgrades for personal convenience.

## 4. Pre-Approval Requirements
Any single expense over **$250** requires manager pre-approval unless it is booked through an approved company process. Travel outside the employee's normal work region also requires prior approval.

## 5. Receipt Requirements
Employees must submit itemized receipts for any expense of **$25 or more**. Missing receipts may result in delayed processing or denial unless an exception is approved.

## 6. Submission Deadline
Reimbursement requests must be submitted within **30 calendar days** of the expense date. Late submissions may be denied unless there is a documented business reason for the delay.

## 7. Submission Process
Employees must submit reimbursement requests through the approved finance or expense management system. Each request should include:
- date of expense,
- amount,
- business purpose,
- category,
- supporting receipt where required.

## 8. Reimbursement Timing
Approved reimbursements are typically paid within **10 business days** after final approval and complete documentation.

## 9. Fraud and Misuse
Submitting false, inflated, duplicate, or misleading expense claims is considered misconduct and may result in disciplinary action.

## 10. Questions
Questions about this policy should be directed to Finance or Human Resources.
